Masala Paneer

Description

Paneer Masala is a classic North Indian dish featuring succulent cubes of cottage cheese simmered in a robust, spiced tomato-onion gravy. Unlike the sweeter "butter masala," this version focuses on a savory and tangy profile, achieved by slow-sautéing ginger, garlic, and green chillies with aromatic ground spices like cumin, coriander, and garam masala

Ingredients

  • Paneer (cubed)
  • Onions & Tomatoes
  • Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • Green Chillies
  • Oil / Ghee
  • Basic Spices (Salt, Turmeric, Chilli, Garam Masala)
  • Fresh Coriander

Steps

  1. Prep Paneer: Cut the paneer into cubes and soak them in warm water for 10 minutes to keep them soft
  2. Temper Oil: Heat oil or ghee in a pan and add cumin seeds and whole spices (like a bay leaf) until they sizzle.
  3. Sauté Aromatics: Add finely chopped onions and sauté until they turn a deep golden brown.
  4. Add Paste: Stir in the ginger-garlic paste and slit green chillies, cooking for a minute until the raw smell goes away.
  5. Cook Tomatoes: Add tomato puree and salt; cook on medium heat until the oil begins to separate from the sides.
  6. Spice Mix: Lower the heat and stir in turmeric, red chilli powder, and coriander powder, adding a splash of water so they don't burn.
  7. Final Simmer: Add the paneer cubes and a little warm water; simmer for 5 minutes, then finish with a pinch of garam masala and fresh coriander.
